1st amendment - ... Which By Their Very Utterance ... by Lexa Walsh

Congress will not make laws involving a religion’s rights to practice or exist, an individual’s ability to write or speak publicly, the rights of the people to protest peacefully and petition the government for its wrongdoings.

The 1st amendment is ripe with a history of rich sound reflected in its use and is a natural candidate for a sound based, educational, playable game. I propose to create The Voices (working title), a playable game/musical instrument/sound station that explores ideas embedded in the 1st amendment. This includes samples from educational audio sources, music and sounds from a variety of religions and spiritual practices, and field recordings from speeches and protests. Additionally there will be a recording capability so users can make and play back their own ‘free speech’. All of these are embedded in a colorful board with large plastic buttons that can be played.

Through playful interaction and the creation of new compositions, the users are able to learn, turn information into music, explore and ponder the 1st amendment and practice part of the amendment itself. I intend for the work to be both accessible and critical, fun, absurd, and pushing the boundaries of our understanding of the 1st amendment. Through the juxtaposition of a variety of samples, the work can inspire the difficult and complex conversations this moment requires.

The game board is a 30” high, 8” deep, 4’ diameter table made of plywood, housing play buttons on the outside, and a Mini PC, a subwoofer & speakers, and an interface inside. It requires stools to sit comfortably but can also be accessible to wheelchair users and standing children. The buttons have pictures inserted to indicate the sound played. The table is programmed with up to 29 samples, including 3-5 unique looped beats, which can continue throughout the duration of the play. Other samples will be trimmed down to different lengths to be played as accents to the beats, with everything controlled by the player. Additionally, there are record and playback buttons for vocal participation. The samples I intend to use are 3 to 30 seconds long, including the following subject matter:

Educational audio on the First Amendment, the free speech/hate speech debate, critiques of social media, conflict resolution trainings, protests, chants, speeches, debates and voices from activists on diverse sides of the spectrum, bits of songs, like School House Rock and Childish Gambino’s ‘This is America’, sounds from religious traditions such as Black spirituals, the Muslim call to prayer and televangelists, sounds from prisons, voices of journalists trying to get attention, news stories, talk radio hosts across the spectrum, Citizens United, conspiracy theories and their fact checkers, advertisements, arguments about social media algorithms, and more. Some sounds are singular, others are looped.

The samples can be played in part, in full, or in rhythmic beats. I will edit them in GarageBand, some to best suit their natural musicality. I will collect these samples from libraries, the internet, and my own personal archive of recordings. The Voice should be accessible around its entirety, and with electricity nearby, preferably in the floor underneath or hidden safely. Having conceived of and built a similar work before, I chose the following design and materials, which have proven playability and function.

2nd amendment - Standoff by Andy Malone

A well regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed.

Aversion Therapy is an interactive sculpture that explores aspects of the Second Amendment via an interactive puzzle which utilizes an accidental musical composition as a timing mechanism. The player presses a button which starts the musical timer mechanism below. The player then has approx. 5 minutes to create a peaceful resolution on the sliding block puzzle by making all of the fingers point away from each other.

Pressing the button activates a servo motor on a gear drive for five minutes. The gears turn two wooden discs. The upper disc is derived from a paper target shot with a revolutionary-era musket (it could fire 3 rounds per minute.) The lower disc resulted from a paper target shot with a modern AR-15 (which can fire 45 rounds per minute.) The paper targets were magnified onto a larger plywood disc. The centers of the bullet holes were plotted onto the wooden disc and replaced with dowels. The dowels trigger mallets on a balafon (or an African wooden xylophone.)

The location of the bullet holes create an “accidental” musical composition which plays while the player attempts to solve the sliding block puzzle on top. I was inspired by the great experimental musician John Cage. His work introduced me to the beauty of chance. I have no formal musical training (other than a few years of piano lessons), but I was intrigued by John Cage’s attempts to create “Music which is free from the composer’s will.”

The sliding block puzzle is reminiscent of a 15-number puzzle. The player manipulates the blocks by sliding them to the open space. The pointing fingers on the sliding puzzle are analogous to the universal hand gesture people make when they pretend to have a gun. Pointing your finger at someone can also be considered accusatory and rude. It makes them the object of scrutiny. In this puzzle, the solution is to avoid this discomfiture by pointing the fingers away from each other. Goal of this puzzle is not to make war, but peace.

El juego Terapia Aversiva es un rompecabezas que toca una canción una vez resuelto. El jugador empieza el juego al presionar un botón. El botón comienza un conteo de 5 minutos, tiempo necesario para completar el rompecabezas. Desliza las piezas de manera en que ellas apunten en dirección contraria a si mismas. 3rd amendment - Cyber Soldiers in Cyber Houses by Vi Trinh

No Soldier shall, in time of peace be quartered in any house, without the consent of the Owner, nor in time of war, but in a manner to be prescribed by law.

The ultimate goal of the piece is to make people more aware of their right to privacy as outlined in the 3rd amendment, and what that means in a post-internet age and the future. Then, push them to become an active participant in shaping future policy that protects both themselves and the people around them, especially people who are the most vulnerable to having their civil rights violated.

The internet, like social structures, like laws, were built by people, and therefore built with implicit bias. Now, more than ever, our most powerful tools are being built and implemented by a very small group of people, and those groups often exclude minorities, people of color, the disabled, and the LGBTQ community.

A lot of this new and upcoming technology already has problems in equitable distribution. Facial analysis has disparity of accuracy between people of different races and harmful social media algorithms often prefer radical content. If the internet, automatic analysis, and A.I. are drawing from a prejudiced data set the internet, automatic analysis, and A.I. will be prejudiced.

A person’s data and information are invaluable, in an increasingly interconnected world it will become the currency of the world, in that sphere, it will be all too easy to let go of civil liberties in pursuit of power and wealth. Like the beginnings of all power slides, like the one we see now, it will affect people differently depending on their socio-economic status. Pay for you own IP, pay to be informed, pay for privacy, pay for security. The first civil liberties to go will be those that belong to the most vulnerable among us.

I hope to impress on the player that we as a society, as a collective, must make laws to codify what we deem is acceptable for this new realm of technology. Like laws and systems, building games are about building a set of rules and structures that dictate how a player should act.

The most straightforward way the audience interacts with the content is through the A.I. However, It’s up to the player how much they listen and absorb while playing the game. The dialog grows from outlining the 3rd amendment in relation to technology, to specific examples, to moral arguments about freedom and security. The overall effect is to wash the information over the player gently.

Each level is attached to a Person of Interest at risk to have their privacy compromised, and the player has the choice to skip the level or leave it half done in order to reach the end before the timer runs out. This is a difficult decision and trade off, especially as you get to later levels because the Person of Interest won’t matter as much personally to the player. It’ll become easier and easier to abandon the Person of Interest. This is a choice that the player will have to make at every level.

The point is that the player should care. They should care about the rights of people close to them and people they don’t even know, even when it’s inconvenient, especially when it’s inconvenient. Because that is what technology gives you, ease and convenience. Technology can be wonderful and can do a lot of good, but it can also do great evil, it amplifies and magnifies our values and our actions. It makes things easy in all ways, except the decisions we have to make about it. It is easy not to try, it is easy not to care, but people cannot fall into the trap of complacency when trusting our rights will be preserved by those who have a vested interest in not doing so.

At the very least I hope to inform and motivate players about these issues and take part in building the future.

El juego Cyber Soldiers in Cyber Houses es un juego que sigue el formato de “yo espío”. Se juega en una computadora usando el mouse y teclado. Los jugadores mueven el mouse sobre la imágen de una habitación llena de objectos. Haz clic en el objecto que crees que está espiándote. Una vez el jugador encuentre todos los objetos espías el juego avanza hacia 32 imágenes de casas. Cada una de las imágenes tiene más dificultad que la previa.

The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and effects, against unreasonable searches and seizures, shall not be violated, and no Warrants shall issue, but upon cause, supported by Oath or affirmation, and describing the place to be searched, and the persons or things to be seized.

Contempt is designed to re-engage with civics education following the discourse around the death of Breonna Taylor. One of the most painful things to see

Spanish Summary

No se infringirá el derecho del pueblo a que sus personas, domicilios, papeles y efectos estén protegidos contra los registros y las incautaciones irrazonables, y no se expedirán a ese fin órdenes que no se justifiquen por un motivo verosímil, que estén corroboradas por juramento o afirmación, y en las que se describa específicamente el lugar que deba registrarse y las personas o los objetos que han de aprehenderse.

El juego Contempt tiene 4 partes. Todas estas partes se juegan al hacer clic en las tablets en la mesa. La primera parte es un juego de palabras. El jugador trata de reorganizar el texto de la cuarta enmienda. Una vez que las palabras están organizadas, el jugador pasa a la segunda parte. Durante la segunada parte del juego el jugador se despierta en un cuarto oscuro. El jugador tiene que averiguar que ha pasado y decidir como responder en menos de 2 minutos. En la tercera parte del juego el jugador escucha dos grabaciones ocurridas en la corte sobre casos que involucran la 4ta enmienda. En la cuarta parte del juego el jugador vuelve a la primera parte con el juego de palabras donde se les da la oportunidad de escribir una versión más justa de la enmienda.

El juego, ___ vs. ___, es un juego de deducción, jugado por 2 jugadores que actúan como “El Sospechoso” y “El Investigador”. Hay 2 mesas con 2 sillas cada una. Cade mesa es para 2 jugadores. El Investigador trata de conseguir una confesión de parte del Sospechoso y El Sospechoso trata de evadir la confesión. Para comenzar, El Sospechoso selecciona 2 cartas de crímenes. El Sospechoso y El Invesigador se ponen de acuerdo con las reglas de la investigación. Estas reglas determinan lo que El Investigador puede y no puede preguntar.

6th amendment - Nomologos by Peter Bradley

In all criminal prosecutions, the accused shall enjoy the right to a speedy and public trial, by an impartial jury of the State and district wherein the crime shall have been committed, which district shall have been previously ascertained by law, and to be informed of the nature and cause of the accusation; to be confronted with the witnesses against him; to have compulsory process for obtaining witnesses in his favor, and to have the Assistance of Counsel for his defense.

Spanish Summary

En cualquier causa penal el acusado gozará del derecho a que se lo juzgue con prontitud y públicamente por un jurado imparcial del Estado y distrito donde se hubiera cometido el delito, cuyo distrito habrá sido fijado por ley; asimismo, a que se le haga saber la índole y causa de la acusación; a que se caree con los testigos en su contra; a que se obligue a comparecer a los testigos en su favor, y a contar con los servicios de un abogado defensor.

El juego, Nomologos, es un videojuego de un solo jugador. Este mueve la bola con el teclado alrededor del Mall Nacional. La dirección es determinada por la palabra escrita en el teclado. Para moverse a la izquierda, el jugador debe escribir un antónimo (definición opuesta) a la palabra en la pantalla. Para moverse a la derecha, el jugador debe escribir un sinónimo (definición similar o igual). Para saltar, el jugador debe escribir un hiperónimo (palabra general). Para bajar, el jugador debe escribir un hipónimo (palabra más específica). Escribir las palabras correctamente causará que la bola se siga moviendo. Si no te mueves por el Mall Nacional, perederás una vida.

Spanish Summary

El juego Verbal Gymnastics es un juego rítmico de 2 jugadores. Cada jugador mueve sus brazos para atrapar bolas en la pantalla, lanzadas por diferentes personajes animados. El jugador debe atrapar bolas de ciertos colores dependiendo de que personaje ha lanzado la bola. El jugador que capture la mayor cantidad de bolas correctas gana.

Spanish Summary

Evolving Standards of Decency es un laberinto cardán. El jugador mueve el laberinto de manera en que la bola se mueva hacia el centro. Para moverse a la izquierda, el jugador inclina el laberinto hacia la izquierda. Para moverse hacia arriba, el jugador inclina el laberinto hacia el/ella mismo/a. Si la bola cae en uno de los hoyos el jugador pierde y debe empezar de nuevo.

10th amendment - V.erses by arts.codes (Melissa Clarke & Margaret Schedel)

El juego v.erses es una columna alta con 9 lados. Cada lado tiene una pantalla de video con sensores. Hay 2 maneras de jugar: La primera es decidir si el estado o el gobierno federal deciden el caso. La segunda es tocar los sensores en cada lado de la columna. Este tocará música de acuerdo con las decisiones del primer jugador y los sonidos seleccionados por el segundo jugador.
