Wheelchair Access

Federal Hall’s entrances and facilities are accessible to wheelchair users. An accessible entrance is located towards the back of the main floor rotunda on 15 Pine St. North. All informational stands and games are at wheelchair level. All restrooms are wheelchair accessible. Restrooms are located on the basement floor, which can be accessed through elevator.

Individuals who are blind or have low vision

Our website is compatible with screen readers. We recommend utilizing the website to learn more about the exhibition.

Games have auditory and tactile feedback, and we use braille in our informational stands and pamphlets. Our personnel is trained to help anyone who requests it.

Individuals who are deaf or hard of hearing

Automated captioning is available for all video content. American Sign Language (ASL) interpretation and live captioning is available for public programs upon request with advance notice. Shall Make, Shall Be will make every effort to provide accommodation for requests made on the same day. Please contact emailhere@gmail.com to make a request for these accommodations.

Individuals with intellectual or developmental disabilities

Federall Hall’s basement provides a quiet area for individuals with intellectual or developmental disabilities. Please refer to the map above to locate all sitting and quiet areas.

Individuals with dementia or Alzheimer’s

Our personnel is trained to help individuals with Dementia or Alzheimer’s Disease. We recommend making use of the sitting and quiet areas frequently and taking breaks when needed.

QR CODES

QR codes are placed throughout the Museum to provide features and resources created to enhance your visit. Here’s how to use them:

iOS Devices

  • Open the Camera app from the Home Screen, Control Center, or Lock Screen.
  • Select the rear facing camera. Hold your device so that the QR code appears in the viewfinder in the Camera app. Your device recognizes the QR code and shows a notification.
  • Tap the notification to open the link associated with the QR code.

    • Android Devices

  • Open the camera app.
  • Select the rear-facing camera in Photo or Camera mode and center the QR code that you want to scan on the screen.
  • Tap the notification that pops up to open the link.

    • If no notification appears, follow these instructions to use Google Lens:

  • Tap the color lens icon in Google Assistant, or open the Google Lens app.
  • Select the Search mode and center the QR code that you want to scan on the screen.
  • Tap the notification that pops up to open the link.
